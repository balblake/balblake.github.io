Biography

I am an Electrical Engineering student at Texas A&M University minoring in Mathematics, with an expected graduation in May 2028. My academic focus spans Power Systems, semiconductors, and microcontrollers.

Through coursework, an academic internship, and independent projects, I have built a strong foundation in both programmatic and hardware problem-solving. I leverage my experience analyzing complex datasets and programming various FPGAs to engineer tangible solutions, including embedded security systems, traffic control logic, and data acquisition tools.

About Me

Beyond the lab and the lecture hall, a significant part of my life was dedicated to the marching arts. I have spent multiple seasons performing at a world-class level with drum and bugle corps, including serving as the Bassline Section Leader for the Blue Stars.

The drum corps activity requires an extreme level of physical discipline, exact timing, and thousands of hours of repetitive refinement to achieve perfection on the field. I also apply these principles as a Drumline Technician, instructing high school percussionists. The leadership, resilience, and attention to detail I've cultivated in the marching arts directly translate to my engineering work—teaching me how to break down complex, multi-layered problems and lead a team toward a unified goal.
