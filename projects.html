FPGA Digital Systems & Hardware Validation

August 2025 - December 2025 View on GitHub →
[ Video / Image Placeholder: Board functioning or Vivado Schematic ]

The Objective

To design, simulate, and deploy a robust multi-state Finite State Machine (FSM) onto a physical FPGA to control real-world components, specifically mimicking a multi-directional traffic intersection.

The Approach

I utilized Behavioral and Structural Verilog to manage complex timing sequences. The logic was synthesized via Vivado and flashed to a ZYBO Z7-10 board. I integrated clock dividers to step down high-frequency system clocks into observable, real-time physical delays.

For hardware validation, I mapped the FPGA output signals to a breadboard to drive LED indicators and physically characterized the external components by measuring I/V curves with an oscilloscope.
